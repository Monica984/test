About NDRF

The National Disaster Response Force coordinates specialized response for natural and man‑made disasters across India — search and rescue, medical aid, logistics, and capacity building with communities and institutions.

What they do

  • Urban Search & Rescue (collapsed structures, confined spaces)
  • Flood & Cyclone response (swift‑water rescue, evacuation)
  • CBRN incident response (Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear)
  • Medical first response & relief logistics
  • Community awareness & joint mock drills

How to contact in an emergency

  1. Dial local helplines: 112 (ERSS), 100/101/102 as applicable.
  2. Follow your State/UT disaster management authority advisories.
  3. Use verified channels for updates; avoid sharing rumours.

Disaster Safety Tips

Quick guidance for common hazards. Expand each item.

  • Move to higher ground; avoid walking/driving through floodwaters.
  • Turn off electricity/gas if instructed; keep emergency kit ready.
  • After waters recede, avoid stagnant water and downed wires.
  • Secure windows/doors; bring loose items indoors.
  • Stay away from glass; keep devices charged and a battery radio.
  • Heed evacuation orders; do not return until cleared.
  • Drop, Cover, Hold On under sturdy furniture.
  • Stay indoors until shaking stops. If outside, move away from buildings.
  • Prepare a family plan and identify safe spots in each room.
  • Hydrate frequently; avoid strenuous activity in peak hours.
  • Never leave children/elderly/pets in parked vehicles.
  • Know the signs of heat exhaustion and heat stroke.
